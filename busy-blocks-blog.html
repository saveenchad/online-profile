The clock ticked to 6:20 PM signaling to everyone that class was finally over. The class slowly shuffled outside into the crisp, spring, evening air. Excited chatter began to fill the open space outside of the classrooms in Pepper Canyon Hall. One of my friends looks over and says "I've got a great idea for this class" and the rest was history.

The idea as it was pitched was a website that integrated a calendar feature with a social media aspect. It would give the user the ability to plan group events with ease as it would overlay multiple users' calendars in order to find free time among a group. Users would also be able to friend other users and share their schedules. I wasn't immediately on board with the idea as it seemed a little excessive but I thought it would be a great project to work on in my free time.

The team was eventually settled upon with a majority of the members being my roommates, neighbors, and friends. We all decided to get some dinner and pick a team name that best describes us. One of my teammates began choosing random pop culture terms and jokingly expanded the letters into various phrases. We laughed until he came up with the perfect name. Henceforth, we branded ourselves as Team HIPSTER - (Humans in Programming, Science, Technology, Engineering, and Research)

As a part of the course (CSE 110), each member of the group was assigned a job title which correlated to the work they did on the team. My title was Software Engineer Lead. I chose this position because I felt like I had a lot of web development experience at the time and my time would best be used managing others and getting high level functionality working. This also let me have a say in all decisions made about the application as a whole. One of my core responsibilities was to make and present the System Requirements document. This document outlined the functionality of the web app by detailing what the system shall and shall not do in various scenarios.

Unfortunately, with only 10 weeks, not everyone was able to directly contribute to the programming of the website. Three other teammates and I devoted our time to the code while the other seven members worked on the documentation. As the quarter came to an end, I couldn't help but feel accomplished by how many new technologies I had learned: AngularJS, Parse, Git, etc.

At the end of quarter, we made a video to highlight the feataures of our webapp. We presented to our discussion group of three other teams and won with the most votes. We moved up to the class tier and presented against the winners of the other discussion groups. Here, we came in 4th place of the 16 teams.
